The basics

Phishing is a con, not a hack.

Phishing isn't about breaking through your phone's security. It's about catching you in a busy moment. A message that looks like it's from your bank, a delivery service, or a coworker, arriving right when you're distracted, is often all it takes. It works because of timing and trust, not because you weren't careful enough.

Text phishing (smishing)

A text arrives that looks like it's from a delivery company, your bank, or even a family member, usually while you're mid-scroll, driving, or in the middle of something else. It creates urgency and pushes you toward a link that leads to a fake page built to collect your information.

SMS · iMessage

Website & deepfake phishing

A cloned website can look identical to your bank's real login page. Increasingly, attackers go further with a deepfake video embedded on the fake site, a synthetic "support agent" built to earn your trust in the few seconds you have to decide.

Web · Video

How a phishing text plays out

The same four moves show up in almost every smishing attempt. Cybersafe is built to flag it at step three, before you act on it.

01

The message

A spoofed message arrives, faked to look like a delivery service, bank, or contact you recognize.

02

The pressure

Urgent language, like "act now," "account locked," or "package held," designed to make you act fast, before you've had a moment to think it through.

03

The link

A link leads to a page built to look like the real thing, often live for only a few hours.

04

The result

Login details, card numbers, or personal data entered on the fake page go straight to the attacker.

How a phishing website or deepfake plays out

Website and deepfake phishing follow a similar arc, but lean on visual and vocal trust instead of just text.

01

The setup

A cloned login page, or a deepfake video on a fake site made to look and sound like someone real.

02

The approach

You're directed there through a text link, a search ad, or a social media message.

03

The mask

Familiar branding, a real logo, or a deepfake video that matches someone you already trust.

04

The ask

A form, a "verification" code, or a payment request. This is the real goal behind the disguise.
